Pricing & Comparisons

What Does an Airbnb Co-Host Pay for Cleaning in Davenport FL? (Real 2026 Rates)

By Luana·2026-05-05·7 min read

Co-hosts in Davenport FL — the property managers who run multiple Airbnb listings on behalf of owners — operate at a different cost structure than individual hosts. They negotiate volume rates, often have direct vendor relationships, and treat cleaning as a managed expense rather than a per-booking line item. This article breaks down what co-hosts in the Davenport / ChampionsGate / Reunion corridor actually pay for cleaning in 2026, and how the pricing differs from one-off host pricing.

Single-Host Pricing vs Co-Host Pricing

What Co-Hosts Actually Negotiate

Volume discount is only one of several variables a co-host typically negotiates with a Davenport cleaning vendor:

  • Net 15 or net 30 invoicing instead of per-clean payment — improves cashflow when managing 10+ turnovers per week
  • Automatic scheduling integration — the cleaner pulls bookings from a shared calendar (iCal, PMS like Hostfully or Guesty)
  • Photo report standard — every turnover comes with timestamped photos to the co-host before the next guest arrives
  • Same-day turnover guarantee — typically with a 2-3 person crew window guaranteed when checkout-to-checkin is under 5 hours
  • Linen swap option — vendor provides clean linen sets and rotates dirty ones to laundry, removing one logistical step from the co-host's plate
  • Damage and inventory reports after every turnover, in a standardized format

The Real Cost Structure for a Davenport Co-Host

For a co-host managing 8 properties at 70% occupancy in the Davenport corridor:

  • ~530 turnovers per year across the portfolio
  • Average turnover cost: ~$165 (4BR mix at co-host rate)
  • Annual cleaning expense: ~$87,500
  • Linen service: ~$15,000 additional
  • Restocking and damage upcharges: ~$5,000
  • Total cleaning operations: ~$107,500/year

Compared to the gross revenue across that portfolio (~$650,000-$800,000/year for 8 properties at the right rates), cleaning is roughly 13-17% of total revenue. It's the single largest variable operating expense after Airbnb fees, often larger than cleaning agreement penalties or even property management commissions on a percentage basis.

What Co-Hosts Should Avoid

  • Multiple cleaning vendors per portfolio — fragments the relationship, kills volume leverage, increases coordination time. Pick one or two reliable vendors for the entire portfolio.
  • Per-clean payment — wastes administrative time on invoicing. Net-15 with consolidated weekly invoicing is standard.
  • Cleaning fees that pass-through 100% to guest — this leaves no margin for damage upcharges, late cancellation fees, or restocking. Most experienced co-hosts charge guest cleaning fees ~10-15% above the actual cost they pay the vendor.
  • Vendors without backup capacity — when a vendor's primary crew is sick or unavailable, the entire portfolio can fail same-day turnovers. Confirm backup crew capacity before signing on.

Davenport-Specific Considerations

The Davenport / ChampionsGate / Solterra corridor has specific operational characteristics co-hosts should factor in:

  • Larger average property size (4-6 bedrooms is typical for resort homes) means turnover cost-per-unit is higher than Kissimmee or Orlando
  • Pool and spa cleaning is almost always required as part of the turnover scope
  • Game rooms and theater rooms add 30-45 minutes to a turnover and should be priced accordingly
  • HOA-managed communities (ChampionsGate, Reunion) sometimes have specific cleaner credentialing requirements
