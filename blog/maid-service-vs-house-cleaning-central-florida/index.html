Maid Service vs House Cleaning in Central Florida — What's the Real Difference? (2026)

By Braza Cleaning Team·2026-04-23·10 min read

If you've searched for professional cleaning in Central Florida, you've seen both terms: maid service and house cleaning. Most companies use them interchangeably. But they describe two different service models — and knowing the difference helps you choose what you're actually paying for.

What Is Maid Service?

Maid service means recurring professional cleaning by a consistent, assigned team. The same people come to your home on a regular schedule — weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly — and they learn your preferences, your priorities, and your home's specific characteristics over time.

This relationship element is what distinguishes maid service. Your assigned maids know that you prefer unscented products, that the ceiling fan in the master bedroom needs dusting every visit, that the dog stays in the laundry room during cleaning. None of that develops through one-time services — it's the product of an ongoing professional relationship.

  • All bedrooms dusted, vacuumed, and mopped
  • All bathrooms fully scrubbed and sanitized
  • Kitchen counters, stovetop, sink, appliance exteriors, microwave interior
  • Living and dining areas cleaned
  • Trash emptied throughout
  • Beds made and linens straightened

What Is House Cleaning?

House cleaning is the umbrella term for professional residential cleaning in all forms — including recurring maid service, but also:

  • One-time cleaning — for a special occasion, pre-listing clean, or post-event reset
  • Deep cleaning — inside appliances, inside cabinets, grout scrubbing; done before starting recurring service or once or twice annually
  • Move-out cleaning — comprehensive cleaning of an empty home for landlord or buyer standards
  • Move-in cleaning — cleaning a new home before moving in

The Four Key Differences

1. Team Consistency

Maid service: Same assigned team every visit. They know your home.
One-time cleaning: Whoever is available. Different people each booking.

A team that has cleaned your home 20 times is 15–20 minutes faster per visit and delivers better results because they've learned your surfaces and priorities.

2. Pricing

Maid service (bi-weekly): A 3BR home runs $130–$200/visit in Central Florida.
One-time cleaning (same 3BR): $165–$250 — 15–25% more per visit.

The recurring discount exists because scheduling is predictable, the home is already maintained, and the company has a reliable revenue relationship worth investing in.

3. Relationship

Maid service: Ongoing. Your team learns your preferences. Issues addressed proactively.
One-time cleaning: Transactional. You describe needs fresh each time with no prior context.

4. Scope Per Visit

Maid service: Standard recurring scope every visit. Deep cleaning on scheduled intervals.
One-time cleaning: Often more comprehensive initial scope since the team doesn't know your home's condition.

Which Do You Actually Need?

  • Choose maid service if you want consistent professional cleaning on a schedule without having to think about it.
  • Choose one-time cleaning for a specific occasion, move, or if you want to try professional cleaning before committing to recurring service.
  • Choose deep cleaning if the home hasn't been professionally cleaned in 3+ months, or as your first visit before starting a recurring plan.

Most Central Florida homeowners start with a deep clean, then transition to bi-weekly maid service — the best combination of results and ongoing cost.

FAQ: Maid Service vs House Cleaning

Is maid service the same as a cleaning lady?

Informally yes. Professional maid service is the formalized version with licensing, insurance, background checks, and consistent team assignment.

Can I switch from one-time cleaning to maid service?

Yes — most clients start with a one-time deep clean, then transition to bi-weekly maid service at a lower recurring rate.
