Properties here range from 2-bedroom townhomes to 10-bedroom themed estates, and the cleaning fee guests see at checkout varies significantly by home size.

How Do You Calculate the Right Cleaning Fee for Your Davenport Vacation Home?

Your cleaning fee has one job: cover what it actually costs to turn over your property between guests — and then a bit more for peace of mind. The formula is straightforward:

Cleaning Fee = Professional cleaning cost + restocking allowance + laundry (if separate) + coordination buffer

Never set the fee below what you pay your cleaner. That means absorbing the gap out of your nightly revenue on every single booking — a margin leak that compounds fast over a busy Davenport season.

Here is how the cost basis looks for a typical 4-bedroom pool home in ChampionsGate in 2026:

  • Professional turnover cleaning (flat rate): $165–$185
  • Restocking allowance (toiletries, paper products, kitchen essentials): build in as ~5–8% of ADR
  • Linen laundry: either included in your cleaner's quote or quoted separately — confirm before first booking
  • Coordination buffer (~5% margin): protects you from absorbing small rate increases mid-season

The widely used rule of thumb is to set your cleaning fee at 25–50% of your average daily rate (ADR). For a 4-bedroom home averaging $240/night in Davenport, that puts your ideal fee between $165 and $230 — consistent with the market table above. If the formula lands below your actual cleaning cost, use your cleaner's quoted price as the floor and accept a slightly higher fee-to-ADR ratio. The math is still sound; Davenport guests in resort communities are accustomed to meaningful cleaning fees on larger homes.

Does a High Cleaning Fee Hurt Bookings at ChampionsGate or Solterra?

This is the question every Davenport host asks before setting their fee — and it has a nuanced answer. A cleaning fee that is too high relative to nightly rate can suppress short-stay bookings. A cleaning fee that is too low creates a financial problem (you absorb the difference) or a quality problem (you hire a cheaper cleaner). Here is what matters most in Davenport's resort market:

  • The 25–50% rule works here. Vacation rental guests booking ChampionsGate or Windsor Hills are experienced Airbnb users. They expect and accept a meaningful cleaning fee on a large pool home. Listings where the cleaning fee falls within 25–50% of the nightly rate convert at the highest rates in this market.
  • Minimum stays are your best lever. Most Davenport resort home hosts enforce a 3- to 5-night minimum. Spreading a $220+ fee across 4 or more nights makes the per-night cleaning cost feel proportionate to guests comparing total price at checkout.
  • Total price is what guests actually see. Airbnb displays total price (nightly rate × nights + cleaning fee + taxes) in search results by default. A $200 cleaning fee on a $280/night, 5-night booking represents a modest addition to the total — far less damaging to conversion than a single bad cleanliness review that tanks your search ranking for the next 30 days.

The real booking risk is not a correctly priced fee. It is an under-cleaned home that earns a 3-star cleanliness rating and falls off Airbnb's first page in Osceola and Polk County searches.

What Is Included in the Turnover Clean Behind Your Davenport Cleaning Fee?

Guests staying in resort homes at ChampionsGate, Solterra, or Windsor Hills arrive with high expectations — they are paying premium rates for a Disney-area vacation and they scrutinize cleanliness reviews before booking. A professional Airbnb turnover clean at Davenport market rates covers every surface a guest will see, touch, or use:

  • All bathrooms: toilets, showers, tubs, sinks, and mirrors sanitized; floors mopped; towels replaced with fresh set
  • Kitchen & dining: all counters wiped, stovetop deep-cleaned, appliance exteriors, sink sanitized, dishwasher emptied and reset, floors mopped
  • All living areas: dusting of surfaces and ceiling fans, floors vacuumed and mopped, furniture wiped
  • All bedrooms: beds stripped and remade with fresh linens, mirrors and surfaces wiped, floors vacuumed
  • Game rooms & themed rooms: surfaces wiped, equipment reset, floors cleaned (standard in Davenport resort homes)
  • Pool deck & lanai: outdoor furniture wiped, floor swept, pool towels replaced (pool chemistry is a separate pool service vendor)
  • All trash & recycling: emptied and relined throughout the home
  • Restocking check: toiletries, paper products, and kitchen essentials verified; host notified of gaps
  • Damage walkthrough: photo documentation of any breakage or maintenance issues sent to host after every turnover

A critical detail specific to Davenport's large resort homes: linen and towel laundry. For 6–8 bedroom estates, on-site laundry is often run during the turnover to avoid a full linen-swap inventory. Off-site laundry is available as a quoted add-on. Confirm which approach your cleaner uses before your first booking — it affects total turnover time and should be factored into your cleaning fee.

When Should You Update Your Davenport Airbnb Cleaning Fee?

Most Davenport hosts set their cleaning fee at listing launch and forget it — until their cleaner raises rates and they start absorbing the difference. Set a calendar reminder for at least two reviews per year, and update immediately when any of these five triggers occur:

  1. Your cleaner raises their rate. Florida's cleaning labor market has tightened consistently since 2022. When your cleaner notifies you of a price increase, update your Airbnb listing within the same week. Confirmed reservations are unaffected; new bookings will reflect the corrected fee immediately.
  2. You add a pool, hot tub, or game room. Each added amenity increases turnover time. A hot tub requires a drain inspection, wipe-down, and reset between every guest. Discuss the impact with your cleaner and adjust your fee to match the new quote.
  3. Your occupancy rate exceeds 75%. Strong demand means your listing can absorb a higher cleaning fee without materially affecting bookings. Test a modest increase during peak Disney corridor season (June–August and December–January) and monitor conversion rate for two weeks.
  4. You shift to a longer minimum stay. If you move from a 2-night minimum to a 4- or 5-night minimum, guests understand and accept a higher absolute cleaning fee because the per-night effective cost remains proportionate.
  5. After hiring a better cleaner. If you are upgrading your service to address cleanliness review issues, raise the cleaning fee to match the new rate. A $230 fee tied to consistent 5-star reviews is a stronger listing than a $165 fee tied to spotty 3-star scores.
