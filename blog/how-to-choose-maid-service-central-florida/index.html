How to Choose a Maid Service in Central Florida (2026) — Questions That Actually Matter

By Braza Cleaning Team·2026-04-23·10 min read

Choosing a maid service in Central Florida is harder than it looks. There are hundreds of options — national chains, local companies, individual cleaners on apps — and price differences can be enormous. This guide cuts through the noise with the questions that actually reveal whether a service will consistently deliver.

Evaluate in the Right Order

Most homeowners evaluate maid services wrong — they start with price. The result: they hire the cheapest option, get mediocre results, and pay more long-term by switching repeatedly. The right order:

  1. Licensing and insurance — baseline requirement
  2. Team consistency — same people every visit
  3. Satisfaction guarantee — how do they handle problems?
  4. Background check policy — verified, not just claimed
  5. Pricing structure — flat-rate vs hourly
  6. Price — compare last, after the above are confirmed

The Single Most Revealing Question

"Will I have the same team every visit?"

The answer tells you more about quality than anything else.

Good answer: "Yes — we assign a dedicated team to your home. The same people come every visit unless there's an emergency, in which case we notify you in advance."

Red flag: "We try to send the same team when possible." Or: "We have teams in your area that rotate."

A team that has cleaned your home repeatedly delivers results that improve over time. A rotating crew treats each visit as a first visit — slower, less thorough, inconsistent.

Licensing and Insurance — The Non-Negotiable

Professional cleaning businesses in Florida should carry:

  • General liability insurance: Covers damage to your home. If they break something, this pays for it.
  • Workers compensation: If a team member is injured in your home, workers comp covers it — not your homeowners insurance.
  • Business license: Verifiable at Florida's Sunbiz.org.

Ask for a certificate of insurance showing both. Legitimate businesses provide this without hesitation. If there's reluctance — walk away.

The Satisfaction Guarantee Question

"How do you handle problems if I'm not satisfied?"

What you want to hear: "We have a 100% satisfaction guarantee. If anything is missed, call us within 24 hours and we come back and re-clean at no charge."

Yellow flag: "We'll try to make it right" — vague, uncommitted.

Red flag: Defensiveness or "we've never had complaints."

Flat-Rate vs Hourly Pricing

Hourly pricing creates misaligned incentives: the slower the team works, the more you pay. Flat-rate pricing aligns incentives — the company is paid the same whether the clean takes 2 hours or 4 hours.

Right answer: "We quote a flat rate for your specific home based on size, bedrooms, and bathrooms. You know the cost before we arrive."

Red flag: Any "$X per hour" quote without a capped estimate.

National Chain vs Local Service in Central Florida

  • National chains (Molly Maid, Two Maids): Strong brand accountability, standardized training, reliable insurance. Higher price, less flexibility, team consistency can be inconsistent due to turnover.
  • Local licensed services: Often more flexible, more competitive pricing. The best ones outperform chains on consistency and accountability. The worst are significantly worse — the evaluation questions above separate them.

How to Evaluate a Trial Visit

Use the first deep clean as a genuine evaluation:

  • Check bathroom grout — scrubbed or just wiped?
  • Microwave interior — cleaned or just exterior?
  • Floor corners — any dust or pet hair remaining?
  • Mirrors in good light — streak-free or smeared?
  • Team communication — did they introduce themselves, confirm completion?

If the first visit impresses you, recurring service will maintain that standard. If it's mediocre, don't expect improvement — move on.

FAQ: Choosing a Maid Service in Central Florida

How much should I expect to pay for quality maid service?

For licensed, insured, consistent maid service in Central Florida: $115–$200/visit for a 2–3BR home on bi-weekly service. Prices below $90/visit for a 3BR home in a populated market are a strong indicator of unlicensed or uninsured operation.

Can I trust Google reviews to evaluate maid services?

Look for specifics: reviews mentioning the team by name, specific cleaning details, and consistent patterns across 50+ reviews averaging 4.8+. This is meaningfully different from 8 reviews at 4.5.

How do I switch services without a cleaning gap?

Give your current service the required notice (typically 2–4 weeks). Book the new service to start the week after your last visit with the old one. No gap needed.
