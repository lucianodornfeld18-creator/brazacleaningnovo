Airbnb Cleaning

Top 7 Airbnb Cleaning Mistakes Kissimmee Hosts Make (And How to Fix Them)

By Braza Cleaning Team·2026-04-07·10 min read

Running a profitable Airbnb in Kissimmee FL is harder than it looks — and the hosts who struggle almost always have the same underlying problem: preventable cleaning mistakes that drive bad reviews. This guide covers the 7 most common cleaning mistakes Kissimmee vacation rental hosts make, exactly how each one damages your listing, and precisely what to do to fix it.

Mistake #1: Using a Generic House Cleaner Instead of a Vacation Rental Specialist

This is the most common and most damaging mistake. A general house cleaner maintains a home — a vacation rental turnover specialist resets it to hotel standard between guests. These are fundamentally different services. A house cleaner who also does vacation rentals "on the side" typically follows a residential cleaning routine that skips critical guest-facing details: linen presentation, amenity restocking, final walkthrough photography, and damage documentation.

The fix: Use a cleaning service that specializes in vacation rental turnovers — ideally with direct experience in Kissimmee resort communities like Reunion Resort, Storey Lake, and Windsor Hills.

Mistake #2: Skipping Monthly Deep Cleans

Standard between-guest turnovers are essential — but they don't address grout, inside appliances, mineral buildup, or the areas behind and under furniture that accumulate grime over weeks of high-volume use. Kissimmee vacation rentals that run 15–25 turnovers per month without monthly deep cleans develop compounding buildup that eventually becomes visible to guests — and gets reflected in cleanliness reviews.

The fix: Schedule a professional deep clean once per month, and always after any stay of 7+ nights. This prevents cumulative buildup from becoming a guest complaint.

Mistake #3: No Photo Documentation After Guest Checkout

Guests occasionally damage property — stained mattresses, broken fixtures, missing items — and Airbnb's AirCover damage protection requires documented evidence to process a claim. Without timestamped photos taken immediately after checkout (before the cleaning team touches anything), you often have no case. Many Kissimmee hosts have lost hundreds or thousands of dollars in unacknowledged guest damage for this reason alone.

The fix: Require your cleaning service to photograph every room immediately upon arrival after guest checkout, before cleaning begins, and send those photos to you with a timestamp. A professional vacation rental cleaning service should provide this as standard.

Mistake #4: Not Having Same-Day Turnover Capability

Kissimmee's peak season means back-to-back bookings with 4–6 hour windows between checkout and check-in. Hosts who can't reliably turn their property in that window either have to decline bookings (lost revenue) or risk guests arriving to an uncleaned property (a catastrophic review). Many Kissimmee hosts only discover their cleaning service can't handle same-day turnovers when they're already committed to a back-to-back booking.

The fix: Before committing to any cleaning service, specifically ask: "Can you turn a [X]-bedroom property in Kissimmee with 4 hours notice?" and "Are you available on weekends and during Disney peak seasons?" Get the answers confirmed before you rely on them.

Mistake #5: Inconsistent Cleaning Standards Between Visits

Rotating different cleaners through your Kissimmee vacation rental produces inconsistent results — one visit is excellent, the next misses the bathroom grout, the third forgets to restock shampoo. This inconsistency shows up in your reviews as a pattern of "sometimes great, sometimes not" which damages your overall cleanliness score over time.

The fix: Use a cleaning service that assigns a consistent, dedicated team to your property rather than sending whoever is available. A team that cleans your property repeatedly learns its specific quirks and standards over time.

Mistake #6: Ignoring Odor

A Kissimmee vacation home can look spotlessly clean and still receive a poor cleanliness review if it smells off. Pet odors from previous owners, musty air from a closed-up property, cooking smells, or pool chemical buildup in the laundry area all create immediate negative guest reactions — even before they've examined a single surface. Many hosts focus entirely on visual cleanliness and completely overlook odor management.

The fix: Ensure your cleaning team opens windows during the turnover to air the property out. Address any sources of odor directly — pet deposits on upholstery require enzyme-based treatment, not just surface spray. Run the air conditioning during the cleaning so it's circulating fresh air before guests arrive.

Mistake #7: Not Briefing Your Cleaning Team on Property-Specific Issues

Every Kissimmee vacation rental has specific problem areas — a bathroom with hard water staining that requires extra descaling time, a game room floor that shows dirt particularly easily, a pool deck with textured tile that traps debris. If your cleaning team doesn't know about these areas, they won't give them extra attention. Generic cleaning produces generic results.

The fix: Create a property-specific cleaning brief for your team that identifies the areas requiring extra attention in your specific Kissimmee vacation home. Review and update it periodically as you notice new patterns in guest feedback.
