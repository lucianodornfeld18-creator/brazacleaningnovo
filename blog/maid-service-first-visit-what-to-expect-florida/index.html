Your First Maid Service Visit in Central Florida — What to Expect (2026)

By Braza Cleaning Team·2026-04-23·10 min read

Your first maid service visit sets the tone for the entire ongoing relationship. Getting it right — knowing what to expect, how to prepare, and what to evaluate — determines whether you end up with a service that consistently delivers or one that disappoints from the start.

What Actually Happens on Your First Visit

The first visit is categorically different from subsequent recurring visits. Two things make it different:

It's a deep clean, not a standard clean. Professional maid services treat the first visit as a comprehensive reset:

  • Inside all appliances — oven, refrigerator, microwave
  • Inside all kitchen and bathroom cabinets
  • Grout scrubbing in all bathrooms
  • Full baseboard washing
  • Window sills and tracks
  • Behind and under accessible furniture
  • Ceiling fans and light fixtures
  • Everything in the standard recurring scope

The team is learning your home. On the first visit, the team is navigating an unfamiliar space — learning the floor plan, noting where things are, identifying surfaces that need special attention. This takes additional time but pays off in subsequent visits when they know exactly what they're doing.

How Long Does It Take?

  • 2BR home in average condition: 2.5–3.5 hours
  • 3BR home in average condition: 3–4.5 hours
  • 4BR home or home not professionally cleaned in 6+ months: 4–6 hours

After the first deep clean, recurring bi-weekly visits on the same home typically take 1.5–2.5 hours because the baseline is already maintained.

How to Prepare for Your First Visit

Clear Flat Surfaces

Move personal items — mail, chargers, books, toys — off countertops, tables, and nightstands. The team needs access to surfaces to clean them. A team working around clutter cleans less thoroughly than one with full access.

Secure Pets

Dogs and cats roaming freely slow the team significantly. Confine pets to a specific room or crate, or arrange for them to be out of the home during the first visit. This matters for both team safety and cleaning efficiency.

Communicate Special Instructions Before the Visit

  • Any rooms that are off-limits
  • Fragile or valuable items that should not be moved
  • Product preferences — no bleach, unscented, specific hardwood products
  • Surfaces requiring specific care — marble, travertine, real hardwood
  • Access codes or instructions for locked areas

Communicate these before the visit, ideally in writing — not on the day of.

Should You Be Home?

Yes — for the first visit. Reasons:

  • Priority walkthrough: Show the team what matters most to you before they start
  • Meeting your team: These people will have regular access to your home — meeting them face-to-face establishes a better ongoing relationship
  • End-of-visit walkthrough: Identifying issues while the team is still there is the most efficient way to handle first-visit quality concerns

What to Check After Your First Visit

Bathrooms

  • Grout lines — scrubbed or still discolored?
  • Shower glass — descaled or still cloudy?
  • Toilet under the rim — run your finger
  • Cabinet interiors — wiped?

Kitchen

  • Microwave interior — cleaned or just outside?
  • Stovetop grates — removed and cleaned underneath?
  • Refrigerator interior — cleaned if scoped?

Floors and Surfaces

  • Floor corners — dust or pet hair?
  • Mirrors in good light — streak-free?
  • Baseboards — wiped?

How to Give Feedback

Give feedback within 24 hours — same day is best. Be specific: "The bathroom grout in the master wasn't scrubbed — it still looks gray" is more useful than "the bathroom wasn't clean." A professional service will re-clean specific areas at no extra charge.

What the First Visit Reveals

Beyond cleaning quality, the first visit reveals:

  • Punctuality — did they arrive in the confirmed window?
  • Communication — did they introduce themselves and confirm completion?
  • Professionalism — appropriate equipment, professional appearance?
  • Responsiveness — when you gave feedback, how did the company respond?

FAQ: Maid Service First Visit

Is the first maid service visit always a deep clean?

For professional recurring service, yes. Companies offering the first visit at the same price as recurring visits are either overpricing the recurring rate or providing a less comprehensive first clean. Confirm before booking.

What if I'm unhappy with the first visit?

Contact the company the same day with specific feedback. A professional service returns to re-clean issues at no charge. If the response is dismissive, that tells you everything about the ongoing relationship.

How do I provide home access after the first visit?

Options: spare key kept by the service, lockbox code, or smart lock with a team-specific code. Discuss this after the first visit and before your second scheduled clean.
