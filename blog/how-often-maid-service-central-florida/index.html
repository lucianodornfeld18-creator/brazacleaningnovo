How Often Should You Get Maid Service in Central Florida? (2026 Honest Guide)

By Braza Cleaning Team·2026-04-23·10 min read

How often to get maid service doesn't have a universal correct answer. It depends on your household, your standards, and your specific Central Florida home. This guide gives you an honest framework — not a recommendation designed to maximize a company's revenue.

The Three Standard Frequencies and Who They're Actually For

Weekly — Who Genuinely Needs It

Weekly maid service is right for a specific set of households, not for everyone:

  • 3 or more children at home, especially under 10
  • Multiple large shedding dogs — labs, goldens, huskies, German shepherds
  • Homes over 3,500 sq ft with extensive open-plan space
  • Regular entertaining — home needs to be guest-ready weekly
  • Household members with significant dust or pet allergies
  • High-income households where time recovery has clear financial value

Bi-Weekly — Right for Most Homes

Bi-weekly is the right choice for most Central Florida households:

  • Families with 1–2 children
  • Homes with 1–2 pets of any size
  • 2–4 adult households with normal daily activity
  • Most 2–4BR homes in The Villages, Clermont, Winter Garden, Ocoee, Kissimmee, and Lady Lake
  • Professionals who work outside the home and want a clean home every other weekend

Monthly — When It's Genuinely Appropriate

Monthly gets a bad reputation because people book it for homes that need bi-weekly. When it's actually right:

  • 1–2 adult households, no children, no pets
  • Active adult community residents who maintain homes daily
  • Snowbirds during absence — maintenance cleaning rather than full service
  • Homes under 1,200 sq ft with minimal daily activity

How Central Florida's Climate Affects Cleaning Frequency

  • Humidity and mold: Florida's humidity accelerates mold in bathroom grout, especially June–September. Homes near lakes in Clermont, Leesburg, or Lady Lake are particularly susceptible.
  • Sandy soil: Central Florida's sandy soil tracks indoors easily, particularly with dogs or children going in and out.
  • Spring pollen: February–April pollen season deposits on every horizontal surface. Some homeowners add a mid-cycle wipe during peak pollen weeks.
  • Summer rain and pool use: Wet feet, wet dogs, and tracked mud increase cleaning demands significantly.

The Observation Test — How to Know Your Actual Frequency

After your next cleaning visit, check at day 7 and day 14:

  • Bathroom counters and grout — acceptable condition?
  • Kitchen floor corners — pet hair or crumbs?
  • Master bedroom — visible dust?
  • Living room surfaces — noticeable buildup?

Day 7 uncomfortable = weekly. Day 14 manageable = bi-weekly. Day 14 still excellent = monthly may work.

Seasonal Adjustments — A Smart Central Florida Strategy

  • School year (Sept–May): Bi-weekly — adults at work, kids at school, less daytime activity
  • Summer (June–Aug): Weekly — kids home, pool use, more cooking, more accumulation
  • Snowbird adjustment: The Villages and surrounding residents go to monthly maintenance during absence, then return to bi-weekly on arrival

The First Visit Is Always a Deep Clean

Whatever frequency you choose, the first visit should be a deep clean — more comprehensive than recurring visits. It covers inside appliances, inside cabinets, grout, and baseboards. This establishes a clean baseline. Without it, recurring visits are maintaining a home that was never fully cleaned to professional standards.

FAQ: How Often Should You Get Maid Service

Can I start bi-weekly and switch to weekly later?

Yes — most Central Florida maid services allow frequency changes with 1–2 weeks notice.

Does more frequent maid service mean better results?

Weekly often produces noticeably better results in active households because there's less accumulation per visit. The difference is most apparent in families with children and pets.

Is it worth adding a mid-cycle clean during Florida pollen season?

For allergy sufferers, yes. Adding a surface wipe-down during peak pollen weeks (February–April) as a one-time add-on is more efficient than switching to weekly year-round.
