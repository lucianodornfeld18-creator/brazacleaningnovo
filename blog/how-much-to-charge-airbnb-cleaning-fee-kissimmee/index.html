How Much Should You Charge as an Airbnb Cleaning Fee in Kissimmee FL? (2026 Host Guide)

By Braza Cleaning Team·2026-04-07·10 min read

Setting the right Airbnb cleaning fee in Kissimmee is one of the trickiest pricing decisions a vacation rental host makes. Too high and you suppress short-stay bookings. Too low and you're subsidizing every guest's cleaning cost out of pocket. This guide gives you the real numbers and strategy experienced Kissimmee hosts use to price cleaning fees that work for their bottom line and their booking rate.

What Airbnb Cleaning Fees Look Like in Kissimmee Right Now

Based on active Kissimmee listings in 2026, here's what guests are currently paying:

  • 2BR/2BA condos and townhomes: $75–$120 per stay
  • 3BR/2BA pool homes: $100–$165 per stay
  • 4BR/3BA resort homes: $130–$205 per stay
  • 5BR resort homes: $150–$255 per stay
  • 6–8BR large resort properties: $200–$360 per stay

These are guest-facing fees — not what the cleaning actually costs. The gap between cleaning fee and actual cost varies widely by host strategy.

Your Actual Cleaning Cost vs. What You Charge Guests

Most Kissimmee hosts don't pass 100% of their cleaning cost to guests — and for good reason. For a 3BR home where turnover cleaning including laundry runs $185–$260 per stay:

  • Full pass-through ($220 fee): Honest, but kills short-stay bookings. A 2-night guest sees $110/night in cleaning alone.
  • 60–70% pass-through ($140–$160 fee): The most common Kissimmee superhost approach. Absorb $60–$80 into the nightly rate. Better conversion on short stays.
  • No cleaning fee, higher nightly rate: Maximizes short-stay appeal but may look expensive for longer bookings. Works best for 1–3 night minimum properties.

How Cleaning Fees Affect Your Airbnb Search Ranking in Kissimmee

Airbnb's algorithm considers total trip cost — nightly rate plus cleaning fee — when ranking listings in search results. This has major implications for Kissimmee hosts competing in a dense market:

  • High cleaning fees significantly suppress 1–3 night bookings — guests see the fee as disproportionate for a short trip
  • Properties with cleaning fees under 15% of nightly rate consistently outperform comparable properties with fees at 25%+ in Kissimmee search results
  • Airbnb's smart pricing tool doesn't account for your cleaning fee when recommending nightly rates — you need to factor this yourself
  • If your occupancy drops noticeably on weekends-only or short stays, your cleaning fee is likely a primary cause

Recommended Cleaning Fee by Property Size in Kissimmee (2026)

These ranges balance cost recovery with booking optimization based on current Kissimmee market performance:

  • 2BR condo: $80–$110 (actual cleaning cost: $150–$220)
  • 3BR pool home: $110–$145 (actual cleaning cost: $175–$270)
  • 4BR resort home: $135–$175 (actual cleaning cost: $200–$310)
  • 5BR resort home: $155–$210 (actual cleaning cost: $230–$380)
  • 6BR+ large resort: $195–$280 (actual cleaning cost: $340–$560)

The difference between what you charge and what you spend is real cost of doing business — account for it in your nightly rate, not by skimping on cleaning quality.

The Impact of Cleaning Fee on Minimum Stay Strategy

Your cleaning fee and minimum stay length are deeply connected in the Kissimmee market:

  • With a 2-night minimum and a $200 cleaning fee, your effective cleaning cost per night is $100 — extremely visible to guests
  • With a 4-night minimum and the same $200 fee, that drops to $50/night — much more acceptable
  • Many Kissimmee hosts who operate high-occupancy properties use longer minimum stays during peak season specifically to make their cleaning fee more competitive on a per-night basis

FAQ: Airbnb Cleaning Fees in Kissimmee FL

What's the average Airbnb cleaning fee in Kissimmee FL?

In Kissimmee FL, average guest-facing cleaning fees range from $75–$120 for 2BR properties and $130–$205 for 4BR resort homes in 2026. Fees above 20% of the nightly rate tend to suppress short-stay bookings.

Should I include my cleaning cost in my nightly rate instead of a fee?

Some Kissimmee hosts do this — it makes short stays more attractive. The tradeoff is that longer-stay guests are effectively subsidized less. Most superhosts use a hybrid: modest fee plus slightly elevated nightly rate.

How often should I reassess my Airbnb cleaning fee?

Review your cleaning fee quarterly, or whenever your actual cleaning costs change. Also review when you notice a drop in short-stay conversion — that's often the first sign your fee is too high relative to market competitors.

Does Airbnb take a cut of the cleaning fee?

Yes — Airbnb charges its service fee on the total guest payment including the cleaning fee. This means your cleaning fee is effectively reduced by the Airbnb guest service fee percentage when calculating your net revenue.
