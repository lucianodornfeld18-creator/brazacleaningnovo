Move-Out Cleaning vs Standard Cleaning in Orlando FL: What Landlords Need to Know

By Luana·2026-05-05·9 min read

Tenants and landlords in Orlando FL routinely confuse move-out cleaning with standard or recurring cleaning, and the cost difference shows up at the worst possible moment — typically when a security deposit dispute is on the line. This guide breaks down the practical differences, what's expected in each, what Orlando landlords typically require, and what tenants should know before scheduling a move-out clean.

What "Standard Cleaning" Actually Covers

👉 Related: Move-Out Cleaning →

A standard cleaning — the kind a recurring service does every week or two — focuses on maintaining a baseline level of cleanliness in a lived-in home. The scope is intentionally limited to what can be done in 2-4 hours:

  • Surface dusting (visible surfaces, furniture tops, sills)
  • Vacuum and mop accessible floor areas
  • Bathrooms: toilets, sinks, tub/shower exterior, mirrors
  • Kitchen: counters, sink, stovetop, exterior of appliances
  • Take out trash, light tidying

What it doesn't cover: inside oven, inside refrigerator, baseboards, walls, behind appliances, blinds, inside cabinets. Those are not part of a standard clean and any cleaner that does them in 2 hours is rushing.

What "Move-Out Cleaning" Actually Covers

Move-out cleaning is a comprehensive deep clean that returns the property to leasable condition. It's typically 5-10 hours of labor for a single-family home and includes everything in a standard clean plus:

  • Inside oven, including racks and broiler pan
  • Inside refrigerator and freezer (defrosted if applicable)
  • Inside dishwasher
  • Inside microwave
  • Inside all kitchen cabinets and drawers (empty/wiped)
  • Inside all bathroom cabinets and vanities
  • All baseboards (hand-wiped)
  • Wall spot cleaning (scuffs, fingerprints)
  • Inside all closets (empty/wiped)
  • Inside windows
  • Window tracks and sills
  • Door frames and door tops
  • Light fixtures and ceiling fan blades
  • HVAC vent covers
  • Patio and lanai sweep (if applicable)

What Orlando Landlords Should Specify in a Lease

The most common dispute at move-out is what level of cleaning is required. To avoid this, Orlando landlords should specify in the lease:

  • "Professional move-out cleaning required, with itemized receipt provided to landlord"
  • "Carpet cleaning by professional service with receipt"
  • "All appliances clean inside and out, including oven and refrigerator"
  • A specific dollar threshold — e.g., "minimum cleaning service of $X" — anchors expectations

What Tenants Should Know to Get a Full Deposit Refund

The Florida Residential Landlord and Tenant Act allows landlords to deduct from the security deposit for cleaning beyond "reasonable wear and tear." Cleaning costs to return the unit to move-in condition almost always qualify. Tenants should:

  • Hire a cleaning service that provides an itemized move-out receipt with date, address, and scope of work
  • Take dated photos of every room after cleaning
  • Do the move-out walkthrough with the landlord present if possible
  • Compare the move-out condition to the move-in condition documentation (photos taken on day one)

A standard clean is rarely sufficient at move-out. Landlords almost always re-deduct from the deposit for the difference between standard and move-out scope, and tenants discover this only when the deposit refund arrives 30+ days later.

Bottom Line for Orlando Renters and Landlords

Standard cleaning maintains a lived-in home. Move-out cleaning is roughly twice the scope and roughly twice the cost. Booking the wrong service at move-out is the most common cause of partial deposit forfeitures in Orlando rental situations. If you're unsure which scope your lease requires, ask the landlord in writing before scheduling.
